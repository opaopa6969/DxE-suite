# DGE Disclaimer and Usage Notes

> Target Gaps: 3-001, 3-002, 3-010, 3-026

---

## 1. Nature and Limitations of DGE

DGE (Design-Gap Exploration / Dialogue-driven Gap Extraction) is **not a comprehensive review methodology**.

- DGE aims to "discover what is unwritten," but does not guarantee the discovery of all gaps
- The absence of a gap in the dialogue does not mean that gap does not exist. Depending on the LLM's knowledge, context window, and character composition, the scope of discovery varies each time
- DGE session results **do not serve as proof of "review completion."** They should be treated as "a record of examination from certain angles"
- A high number of gaps does not indicate high quality. False positives may be included

---

## 2. Not a Substitute for Professional Judgment

DGE output is **not a substitute** for the following professional reviews:

| Domain | What DGE can do | What DGE cannot do |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Security** | Suggest overlooked threat models | Penetration testing, vulnerability assessment, SOC 2 audits |
| **Legal** | Raise legal risk discussion points | Legal advice from attorneys, contract review, regulatory compliance decisions |
| **Quality Assurance** | Inspire test case ideas | Automated testing, CI/CD, load testing, E2E testing |
| **Architecture** | Point out overlooked design considerations | Performance verification, availability measurement, fault injection testing |

When making decisions based on DGE output, obtain confirmation from domain experts.

---

## 3. Notes on LLM-Generated Content

DGE dialogues and gaps are generated by an LLM. The following risks exist:

- **Hallucination**: The LLM may identify gaps based on premises that differ from reality. This can include technically inaccurate observations and exaggeration of nonexistent risks
- **Agreement bias**: LLMs tend to "smooth over" conflicts between characters. Genuinely serious contradictions may be downplayed
- **Knowledge limitations**: Gap discovery capability degrades in domains not covered by the LLM's training data (cutting-edge technologies, niche industry regulations, etc.)
- **Lack of reproducibility**: Even with the same input, results may differ depending on LLM version, provider, and temperature settings

**Human review is mandatory** (method.md Step 2). It is the human's responsibility to read the dialogues and judge the validity of gaps. Performance data shows that 22% of gaps missed by the LLM are additionally discovered during human review.

## 5. Recommended Complementary Methods

DGE is not a self-contained methodology. It is recommended to use it in conjunction with the following:

| Method | Relationship with DGE |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Formal reviews** (ADR, RFC, Design Doc) | Incorporate gaps discovered by DGE into formal design documents |
| **Threat modeling** (STRIDE, DREAD) | Use DGE security sessions as input for threat modeling |
| **Test-driven development / Automated testing** | Convert DGE gaps into test cases and verify in CI/CD |
| **Penetration testing** | DGE is not a substitute for verification by security professionals |
| **User testing / Usability research** | DGE does not substitute for real user behavior |
| **Code review** | DGE operates at the design level. Implementation-level code review is needed separately |
| **Checklist-based review** | DGE excels at discovering "items not on the checklist." Checklists excel at preventing omission of known items. They complement each other |
| **Legal review** | DGE legal gaps should not be used as a basis for legal decisions without attorney confirmation |

---

## Summary

DGE is a methodology for "finding holes in design through dialogue" — nothing more, nothing less. Dialogues are catalysts for thinking, not final judgments. Discovered gaps must be verified by humans, confirmed by experts, and substantiated by testing.
